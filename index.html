How to plan a wedding in 12 months

Most couples plan a wedding in nine to twelve months, and almost all of the stress comes from doing the right task at the wrong time. Venues and photographers book out furthest ahead; stationery, seating and final numbers can wait. The order below is the same one the free printable checklist follows, month by month.

12–10 months out: the things that sell out

Set the budget before anything else — every later decision is a subtraction from it. Then book the two suppliers with the least availability:

  • Agree the total budget and who is contributing what
  • Draft a rough guest count — it decides which venues are even possible
  • Book the venue and lock the date
  • Book the photographer, and the band or DJ if live music matters to you
  • Buy insurance if your venue requires it

9–7 months out: the dress, the food, the save-the-dates

Wedding dresses commonly take six to eight months to arrive and be altered, so ordering here is not early — it is on time. Send save-the-dates once the date and venue are confirmed, especially if guests are travelling or you are marrying abroad.

  • Order the dress and start suit fittings
  • Book the caterer and schedule a tasting
  • Send save-the-dates and reserve room blocks for out-of-town guests
  • Choose the wedding party and tell them what you are asking of them

6–4 months out: the details that make the day feel like yours

  • Book the florist, the cake and transport
  • Order invitations; plan the ceremony readings and music
  • Book the officiant and confirm the legal paperwork for your area
  • Plan the honeymoon and check passport expiry dates

3–1 months out: numbers, seating, rehearsal

Send invitations about eight weeks before the day, with an RSVP deadline three to four weeks before. Caterers usually want final numbers a fortnight ahead — the seating plan cannot be finished until then, so protect that order.

  • Send invitations and track RSVPs as they come in
  • Final dress fitting; break in your shoes
  • Give the caterer final numbers, then build the seating plan
  • Write the vows and speeches; confirm timings with every supplier
  • Hold the rehearsal and hand someone the emergency kit
The one thing worth doing early: write down who is responsible for each supplier on the day itself — not you. A wedding runs on someone else answering the florist's phone call at 9 a.m. while you are still getting ready.

Wedding planning questions people actually ask

How far in advance should you book a wedding venue?

Ten to twelve months for a popular Saturday in summer. Off-peak dates and weekdays open up much later — often inside six months — and usually cost less.

When should save-the-dates go out?

Six to eight months before the wedding, or eight to twelve months for a destination wedding where guests need to book flights and time off.

What is the difference between save-the-dates and invitations?

A save-the-date reserves the day in a guest's calendar and carries no detail. The invitation comes roughly eight weeks before, with the venue, timings and an RSVP deadline.

How long does a wedding dress take?

Typically six to eight months from order to final fitting, since most gowns are made to order and then altered two or three times. Order by around the nine-month mark to keep the alterations calm.

Do I need a wedding planner to stay organised?

No. What you need is one written list, in date order, that you both look at. That is exactly what the free printable checklist above is — and if you want somewhere to keep budgets, vendor contacts, the guest list and your vows together, that is what the full planner is for.

What should be on a wedding checklist?

Venue and date, budget, guest list, ceremony and officiant, catering, photography, attire and alterations, invitations, and a day-of timeline — each anchored to a month before the wedding so nothing gets left to the last week. The free printable above lays them out in date order.

How much should you budget for a wedding?

There is no single right number — decide the total you're comfortable with first, then split it: venue and catering usually take the largest share, followed by photography and attire. Writing the budget down before you book anything is what keeps it from creeping.

When should you send wedding invitations?

Send invitations six to eight weeks before the wedding, and ten to twelve weeks ahead for a destination wedding so guests can plan travel. Save-the-dates go out earlier, around six to eight months out, once the date and venue are locked.
